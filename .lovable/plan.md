

# Art Village Naggar — Website Plan

## Design Direction
A cinematic, slow-scroll editorial website inspired by VMG Hotels Japan. Full-bleed imagery, generous whitespace, quiet typography, and material-first photography. The site should feel like walking through the property — unhurried, textured, and grounded. Warm earth tones (wood amber, stone grey, forest green, fire glow) as the core palette. Minimal UI chrome. Typography-driven with a serif/sans-serif pairing that feels literary, not corporate. Subtle scroll-triggered animations — fade-ins, parallax on images, text reveals.

---

## Pages & Sections

### 1. Homepage — "Stay inside the story."
- **Full-screen hero** with atmospheric image (wood grain, light through Kathkuni window) and the tagline "Stay inside the story." overlaid in quiet type
- **About section** — Two-column layout: the one-paragraph essence ("A 1,000-year-old building technique, still standing, now hosting") with a material close-up image
- **The Three Tiers** — Visual cards for The House, The Shepherd Hostel, and The Cafe, each with a single image and one-line voice descriptor
- **Seasonal section** — Current season highlighted with its mood, colour, and narrative hook (auto-rotating or manually set)
- **Village texture strip** — A horizontal scroll or grid of detail photos (hands, stone, paths, fire stove)
- **Footer** — Contact details, Chachogi coordinates, social links, "The chai will be on."

### 2. Stay — The House (Premium Rooms)
- Full-bleed hero image of a Kathkuni interior
- The architectural story: wall thickness, wood species, earthquake resistance — told in the brand voice (precise, warm)
- Room details presented editorially (not a grid of amenities icons) — each room as a short narrative with images
- iCal booking integration — embedded calendar showing availability, with a booking/inquiry form
- Pricing displayed honestly and simply

### 3. Stay — The Shepherd Hostel
- Same editorial structure but with the hostel's distinct voice: "The cheapest bed in a 1,000-year-old tradition."
- Community ethos, shared meals, radical simplicity
- Pricing, dorm details, iCal availability

### 4. The Cafe
- Menu as storytelling — each dish with its origin story (ingredient, maker, place)
- "Everything on this menu walked here, grew here, or was made by someone whose name we know."
- Open to non-guests messaging
- Atmospheric food-in-context imagery (not styled food photography)

### 5. About / The Story
- **The Three Narrative Layers** presented as a scroll journey:
  1. The Disappearance — Kathkuni is dying (educational, alarming)
  2. The Restoration — Vijyendra's intervention (process, philosophy, before/after spirit without sliders)
  3. The Village — Chachogi, 20 houses, the devta, the people
- Vijyendra's profile — not a bio, a perspective
- The wabi-sabi philosophy, 90% recycled materials, design decisions

## Interactions & Features
- **Smooth scroll animations** — Elements fade and slide in as you scroll, VMG-style
- **iCal booking integration** — Calendar widget on Stay pages showing availability
- **Booking inquiry form** — Name, dates, room preference, message — sends to email
- **Seasonal brand expression** — The homepage adapts its mood (colour accents, hero imagery, tagline variant) based on current season
- **Mobile-first responsive** — The cinematic feel translates to mobile with full-width images and generous vertical rhythm
- **Hamburger navigation** — Minimal top bar with logo and a slide-out menu

## Photography Approach
Unsplash/atmospheric stand-in images for now — focusing on: aged wood textures, stone walls, mountain fog, fire glow, hands working, forest light, simple food on wooden tables. These establish the visual rhythm and can be swapped for real Art Village photography later.
